How It Works

Cryptographic proof at the point of creation.

UTVN signs content the moment it is created, stores the proof immutably, and makes verification available to anyone — forever.

01

Content is submitted

Any piece of content — a statement, article, document, or announcement — is sent to the UTVN signing endpoint.

02

Cryptographic signature generated

The content is hashed with SHA-256 and signed with an Ed25519 private key. The signature is mathematically unique to that exact content.

03

Record stored immutably

The provenance record — content hash, signature, public key, timestamp — is written to a distributed ledger that cannot be altered retroactively.

04

Anyone can verify

Using only the record ID and the public key, anyone anywhere can confirm the content is authentic and has not been altered since signing.

Use Cases

Every institution that depends on trusted information.

UTVN is not built for one industry. It is infrastructure — like DNS or HTTPS — that every domain of institutional life eventually requires.

Journalism

Verified Reporting

Newsrooms sign articles and source materials at the point of publication. Readers and editors can verify that content has not been altered since it was filed.

Government

Authenticated Communications

Official statements, election results, and public health guidance signed cryptographically so citizens can verify they are genuine and unmodified.

Legal

Digital Evidence

Documents, recordings, and communications signed at creation establish an unbroken chain of custody admissible as authenticated digital evidence.

Finance

Market Disclosures

Corporate announcements and regulatory filings signed before distribution. Fabricated market signals become detectable before they cause damage.

Research

Scientific Integrity

Research data and methodology signed at collection. Fabricated studies and altered datasets become provably inauthentic against the original signed record.

Media

Content Provenance

Photographers, filmmakers, and broadcasters sign media at capture. The origin of any piece of content becomes permanently verifiable.

Historical Precedent

Humanity has built this kind of infrastructure before.

Every piece of invisible essential internet infrastructure began as a coordination problem that seemed impossible — until it wasn't.

DNS Domain Name System

A globally distributed, multi-stakeholder system that translates human-readable names into machine addresses. No single entity controls it. It works invisibly and essentially. UTVN follows this architectural precedent.

HTTPS Transport Layer Security

The web's authenticity layer. Two decades ago, used only for financial transactions. Today, universal. Adoption was driven not by regulation but by browsers marking non-HTTPS sites as insecure. UTVN's economic model mirrors this exactly.

GPS Global Positioning System

Originally military infrastructure, opened as a global public good that enabled trillion-dollar industries. The economic value created vastly exceeded the cost of building it. UTVN follows the same arc.
